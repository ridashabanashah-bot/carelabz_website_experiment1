# Claude Design — 4 Homepage Concepts for Carelabs UAE

Attach the **Carelabs logo PNG** and **brand guidelines PDF** to each prompt. Give Claude Design one concept at a time.

## Shared Context (paste this before each concept)

Design a homepage for Carelabs — a power systems engineering, compliance, and risk management company serving the UAE and 50+ countries.

**About:** Carelabs engineers smarter, safer, and future-ready electrical solutions. Core UAE services: Arc Flash Study & Analysis, Short Circuit Analysis, Load Flow Analysis, Relay Coordination Study, Thermographic Inspection, Circuit Breaker Testing, and Cable Testing. Standards: DEWA, IEEE 1584, NFPA 70E, IEC 61482, IEC 60364.

**Mission:** "Redefining power systems with innovation, precision, and sustainability — ensuring businesses operate with efficiency, resilience, and zero compromise."

**Brand colors (strict):**
- Blue: #2575B6 (primary — trust)
- Orange: #F15C30 (accent — energy)
- Dark Blue: #094D76 (deep sections)
- Light Gray: #F2F2F4 (alternating backgrounds)
- Black: #000000 / White: #FFFFFF / Gray: #9C9B9A

**Fonts:**
- Space Grotesk — display headings, nav, and UI
- Inter — body text
- Anton — hero headlines only

**Logo:** "care" in blue + "labs" in orange wordmark. Never stretch, distort, or recolor.

**Navigation:** Sticky navbar. Three items: About Us, Services (dropdown), Contact Us. Contact Us = orange CTA button.

**Homepage sections to include:**
1. Hero — strong headline, subtext, two CTAs (primary orange, secondary outlined)
2. Trust/credentials bar — DEWA, IEEE 1584, NFPA 70E, IEC 61482, IEC 60364
3. Services — 6 core services with titles and descriptions
4. About/value proposition — who Carelabs is, what makes them different
5. How we work / methodology — 3-4 step process
6. Blog/insights preview — 3 recent articles
7. FAQ — 4-5 expandable questions
8. CTA banner — full-width call to action
9. Footer — 4 columns: logo + description, services links, company links, contact info

**Rules:**
- B2B engineering firm — established, precise, trustworthy
- No stock photos of handshakes or generic offices
- No "innovative", "game-changing", "cutting-edge", "best-in-class"
- Mobile responsive
- Real copy, not lorem ipsum

## Concept 1: "The Bechtel" — Cinematic Engineering

**Inspiration: bechtel.com**

Design a homepage that feels like a massive global engineering firm. Think cinematic scale, project-driven storytelling, and quiet confidence.

**Design direction:**
- Full-bleed hero with a dark overlay on a large infrastructure/engineering image — power lines, substations, or industrial switchgear. White text on dark. The hero should feel immersive and wide, like you're looking at a major project site.
- Large bold Anton headline, something like "Engineering Safer Power Systems Across the UAE"
- Below the hero, a horizontal scrolling or grid of project stats: "50+ Countries", "1000+ Projects", "15+ Years" — styled as big bold numbers with small labels underneath
- Services section as large image tiles in a masonry or offset grid — each tile has a dark gradient overlay with white text service name and a subtle arrow
- Methodology section styled as a vertical timeline with alternating left-right content blocks and connecting lines
- About section: full-width image background with a semi-transparent dark overlay and a centered text block
- FAQ: clean accordion, dark background section
- Footer: dark, dense, 4-column, very corporate
- Color balance: 70% dark tones (Dark Blue #094D76, near-black), 20% white sections for breathing room, 10% orange accents on CTAs and highlights
- Typography: big, bold, uppercase Anton headlines. Space Grotesk for subheadings. Inter for body. Lots of letter-spacing on section labels.
- Feel: "We build things that matter." Serious, monumental, zero fluff.

## Concept 2: "The GIA" — Precision & Prestige

**Inspiration: gia.edu**

Design a homepage that feels like a prestigious research institute. Think authority, deep blue elegance, refined typography, and scholarly confidence.

**Design direction:**
- Hero: Deep Blue #094D76 background, clean and uncluttered. No background image — just a perfectly typeset headline in Anton with a subtle geometric accent (thin lines, a grid dot pattern, or a single angular shape). Below the headline, a short description in Inter, and two CTAs.
- Trust bar: Standards displayed in a single row with small shield or badge icons. Very understated, like academic credentials.
- Services: Clean card grid (2x3) on white background. Each card has a thin top border in blue, an icon (Lucide line icon), title in Space Grotesk, and 2-line description in Inter. Cards have a subtle shadow and lift on hover.
- About section: Two-column layout — left column has a large pull quote in Space Grotesk italic with a vertical blue accent line, right column has body text with a "Learn More" link
- Methodology: Numbered steps in a horizontal row, each in its own bordered card. Numbers in large light orange (#F15C30 at 20% opacity), titles in Space Grotesk
- Blog section: Editorial-style — large featured article on the left (takes 2/3 width) with two smaller stacked articles on the right (1/3 width). Clean borders between them.
- FAQ: Light gray background, centered narrow column, elegant accordion
- CTA banner: Blue #2575B6 background (not dark blue — the brighter primary blue) with white text. Feels confident, not aggressive.
- Footer: White or very light gray background with dark text. Clean, minimal. Feels like a research institution footer.
- Color balance: 60% white/light gray, 30% blues (mix of #2575B6 and #094D76), 10% orange on CTAs only
- Typography: Refined, measured. No uppercase on body text. Anton only on the hero headline. Space Grotesk everywhere else for headings with slightly tighter tracking. Inter for body at 18px with generous line height.
- Feel: "We are the authority." Calm, precise, scholarly. Every element has breathing room.

## Concept 3: "The Ramp" — Bold Fintech Energy

**Inspiration: ramp.com**

Design a homepage with bold, modern energy — strong typography, bento-grid layouts, and confident use of color blocks.

**Design direction:**
- Hero: White or very light background. Massive headline in Anton — could span nearly full viewport width. The headline should feel oversized and impactful. Orange accent on one keyword. Below: subtext in Inter, two CTAs side by side. On the right side (or below), a bento-style preview showing 2-3 floating cards/panels that hint at services — like tilted cards with service names.
- Trust bar: Minimal — just the standard names in gray monospace-style text across one line, separated by vertical dividers
- Services: Bento grid layout — 6 services arranged in a 3x2 or asymmetric grid where some cards are taller/wider. Each card has a colored top bar (alternating blue and orange), bold title, short description, and an arrow link. Cards have rounded corners and soft shadows.
- About section: Bold stat callouts in a horizontal row — three big numbers (Space Grotesk, very large, blue) with labels underneath, followed by a paragraph of brand text
- Methodology: Horizontal stepper with connected dots/line. Each step is a card with a number badge, title, and description. Active/hover state fills the dot with orange.
- Blog: Three equal cards in a row, each with a colored category pill, date, title (Space Grotesk bold), and excerpt. Hover lifts the card with a shadow.
- FAQ: White background, slightly rounded accordion items with a light border. Plus/minus toggle icon.
- CTA banner: Full-width, black background with white text and orange CTA button. Bold and punchy.
- Footer: Black or near-black background. 4-column grid. Orange hover states on links.
- Color balance: 50% white, 25% black/dark, 15% blue, 10% orange
- Typography: Big and bold. Anton hero headline at massive scale. Space Grotesk at medium-large for section headings. Inter for body. Don't be afraid of large type sizes.
- Feel: "We move fast and we're good at it." Confident, modern, energetic but still professional.

## Concept 4: "The Mercury × Arup" — Clean & Warm

**Inspiration: mercury.com + arup.com**

Design a homepage that blends Mercury's ultra-clean minimalism with Arup's editorial engineering gravitas. Warm but precise.

**Design direction:**
- Hero: Clean white background with a single accent color moment — a thin gradient line at the top of the page (blue to orange, horizontal), followed by a well-typeset headline in Anton. Subtext in Inter. Two CTAs. No background image, no gradients behind text. Just pure typography. Maybe a single abstract geometric shape in light blue (#2575B6 at 10% opacity) as a decorative element behind the headline.
- Trust bar: Standards displayed as small, cleanly typeset names in gray, inside a thin-bordered container with rounded corners. Very quiet.
- Services: Horizontal list layout (not cards) — each service is a full-width row with a thin bottom border. Left: service number in orange. Middle: title in Space Grotesk. Right: short description in Inter + arrow. Hover state: background shifts to Light Gray #F2F2F4 and arrow slides right.
- About section: Full-width light gray (#F2F2F4) background. Large editorial-style headline (Space Grotesk) on the left, body text on the right. Below: a row of 3 value cards with a left border accent in blue that changes to orange on hover.
- Methodology: Four cards in a single row on a white background. Each card: large faded step number (orange at 15% opacity), title, description. Cards separated by thin 1px vertical lines (not gaps).
- Blog: Three article cards in a row. Each has a thin top accent line in blue, title (Space Grotesk), date in gray, excerpt, and "Read more" link. No images — purely typographic. Clean and editorial.
- FAQ: Centered narrow column on light gray background. Accordion items with no borders — just spacing between them. Open state shows a subtle left border in blue.
- CTA banner: Dark Blue #094D76 background. Centered text, headline in white Anton, description in white/70, two buttons. Generous padding — let it breathe.
- Footer: Warm gray background (#F8F5F0 or similar warm white). Dark text. 4-column grid with thin separating lines between columns instead of gaps. Feels editorial.
- Color balance: 65% white/warm white, 20% light gray, 10% dark blue, 5% orange accents
- Typography: Clean and measured. No uppercase except the Anton hero headline. Space Grotesk at medium sizes for headings — not shouting, just clear. Inter at 16-18px for body with 1.6 line height. Everything feels intentional and calm.
- Feel: "Quietly excellent." Minimal, warm, every element earns its place. The kind of site where you trust the company because the design itself shows restraint and clarity.
