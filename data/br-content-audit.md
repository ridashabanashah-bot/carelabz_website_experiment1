### Analysis of Power Quality is Required in Brazil
All electrical networks face supply disruptions, which are more frequent when storms, natural catastrophes, or accidents destroy power cables. Fluctuations in the power supply are typical.

There are three types of fluctuations, disturbances, and distortions in the properties of power delivered by an electricity network.

**Type 1 — Frequency deviations:** A frequency deviates from its normal frequency range when it surpasses that range.

**Type 2 — Voltage disruptions:** When the voltage form is kept but the voltage amplitude exceeds the typical range, voltage disruptions develop.
- *2.1 Short Duration Fluctuations* — voltage drops (dips) and voltage spikes of up to one minute length.
- *2.2 Long Duration Fluctuations* — under voltages and over voltages lasting longer than one minute.
- *2.3 Unbalanced Voltage* — when the load is distributed unevenly across the phases, resulting in substantial equipment heating.
- *2.4 Neutral-to-Ground Differences* — differences in voltage between neutral and ground.

**Type 3 — Voltage distortion:** Disturbances resulting from voltage distortion occur when the voltage's shape is not uniform or sinusoidal.
- *3.1 Harmonics and Inter-Harmonics* — contribute to device heating, higher losses, inefficient power usage, and resonance issues.
- *3.2 Transients* — lasting less than a tenth of a second, capable of damaging switch contact surfaces, generating system inefficiency, and causing premature failure of motor drives and control.

### Body
Electrical arcs arise when a gas is subjected to a voltage greater than its insulating qualities. When the voltage is strong enough to ionize the air between the conductors, the arc is initiated. When a gas is ionized, it turns into an excellent conductor, enabling current to flow and creating the arc-shaped current known as arc flash. An arc fault is an unintended electrical discharge that produces a fire.

**Arc Flash Effects**
- Elevated temperatures caused by an arc flash produce dazzling light and deafening noise.
- An arc blast is a high-pressure wave that may expel molten metals and fragments.
- Toxic fumes

**Commercial Losses from Arc Flash Dangers**
- Additional expenditures for medical care, rehabilitation, worker's compensation, and costs associated with accident investigation and production downtime.
- Litigation-related legal fees and judgements, penalties, insurance premiums, repair and replacement personnel expenses.
- Negative influence on the company's name and reputation.
- Loss of business possibilities resulting from a history of accidents, which might lead to the revocation of an electrical license.

**Causes of Electrical Arc Flash**
- Insulation of a conductor that has failed
- Human negligence
- Improper maintenance of switches and circuit breakers
- The presence of water close to the equipment is problematic.
- Unplug panels that are occluded.
- High-voltage wiring
- Ionization discharge
- Defective tools and equipment

**Arc Flash Analysis Methodology** — Arc flash risks can arise whenever high-voltage or low-voltage, high-current switching occurs.

**First Step — Determine the Risk of Arc Flash**
- Conduct an arc flash/fault study or analysis, including data collection, software-based power system modelling, short circuit analysis, overcurrent protective device coordination study, arc flash analysis, and arc flash assessment.
- Evaluation of the chance that a worker may be exposed to an arc flash

**Second Step — Manage the Threat**
- Interacting exclusively with de-energized equipment to reduce worker exposure to arc flash dangers.
- Manual systems should be replaced with automated ones.
- Install physical barriers between employees and energized machinery to protect them from potential dangers.

**Third Step — Engineering Control**
- Reduce incident energy by decreasing short circuit current.
- Detection and suppression systems for arc flashes
- Changing and distant controls
- Upgrades or modifications to the switchboard
- Installing permanent circuit breakers
- Insulating material for bus bar
- Alteration of blown fuses

**Fourth Step — Administrative Controls**
- Labeling switch rooms, plants, and other sites to detect incident energy levels.
- Arc border designation
- Documenting arc flash data and diagrams.
- Providing precautionary strategies and processes to reduce exposure
- Implementing incident energy and PPE (Personal Protective Equipment) levels
