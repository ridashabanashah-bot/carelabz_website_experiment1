## Shared Context (paste this before each concept)

Design a homepage for Carelabs — a power systems engineering, compliance, and risk management company serving the UAE and 50+ countries. This is their UAE regional homepage.

**About:** Carelabs engineers smarter, safer, and future-ready electrical solutions. Core UAE services: Arc Flash Study & Analysis, Short Circuit Analysis, Load Flow Analysis, Relay Coordination Study, Thermographic Inspection, Circuit Breaker Testing, and Cable Testing. Standards: DEWA, IEEE 1584, NFPA 70E, IEC 61482, IEC 60364.

**Mission:** "Redefining power systems with innovation, precision, and sustainability — ensuring businesses operate with efficiency, resilience, and zero compromise."

**Brand colors (strict):**
- Blue: #2575B6 (primary — trust)
- Orange: #F15C30 (accent — energy)
- Dark Blue: #094D76 (deep sections)
- Light Gray: #F2F2F4 (alternating backgrounds)
- Black: #000000 / White: #FFFFFF / Gray: #9C9B9A

**Fonts:**
- Space Grotesk — display headings, nav, and UI
- Inter — body text
- Anton — hero headlines only

**Logo:** "care" in blue + "labs" in orange wordmark. Never stretch, distort, or recolor.

**Navigation:** Sticky navbar. Three items: About Us, Services (dropdown), Contact Us. Contact Us = orange CTA button.

**Homepage sections to include:**
1. Hero
2. Trust/credentials bar — DEWA, IEEE 1584, NFPA 70E, IEC 61482, IEC 60364
3. Services — 6 core services
4. About/value proposition
5. How we work / methodology — 3-4 steps
6. Blog/insights preview — 3 articles
7. FAQ — 4-5 questions
8. CTA banner
9. Footer — 4 columns

**Rules:**
- B2B engineering firm — but push the design to feel futuristic, innovative, and memorable
- No stock photos of handshakes or generic offices
- No "innovative", "game-changing", "cutting-edge", "best-in-class" in copy
- Mobile responsive
- Real copy, not lorem ipsum
- Think 2026 web design — not 2020

## Concept 1: "Dark Cinema"

Design a homepage that feels like a high-budget film title sequence. Dark, cinematic, atmospheric.

**Design direction:**

Hero: Full viewport height. Near-black background (#0A1628) with a slow-moving abstract particle grid — tiny dots connected by faint lines, subtly pulsing in blue (#2575B6 at low opacity). The grid represents electrical networks and power systems. On top: the headline in massive Anton uppercase, white, with one keyword in orange that glows subtly. Below: a single-line description in Inter, and one CTA button — solid orange, no outline button. At the very bottom of the hero, a thin horizontal progress line (orange) that fills as the user scrolls down the page, staying fixed at the top after scrolling past the hero.

Trust bar: Invisible background — the dark theme continues. Standards appear as monospaced text in a single horizontal line, separated by glowing orange dots. Very subtle, like system readouts on a control panel.

Services: Still on dark background. Each service is a full-width horizontal row. The left side has a large faded number (01, 02...) in orange at 5% opacity. The title sits in Space Grotesk, white. On hover, a thin orange underline animates from left to right under the title, and a brief description fades in on the right side. No cards, no boxes — just clean typography on dark space.

About section: The first light section. Sharp contrast — white background, dark text. A large pull quote in Anton on the left ("We engineer certainty."), body text on the right in Inter. Below: three stats in a row, each with a large number in Space Grotesk that counts up when it scrolls into view (animated counter). Numbers in blue, labels in gray.

Methodology: Back to dark. Four steps arranged horizontally. Each step is a circle with a glowing border (blue) connected by a thin horizontal line. The active/hovered step's circle fills with orange and its content expands below. Think: sci-fi interface, mission control.

Blog: Dark background. Three articles as minimal text blocks — no cards, no borders. Just: category tag (orange, small caps) → title (white, Space Grotesk) → date (gray). Separated by thin vertical lines. Hover: title shifts to blue.

FAQ: Dark background continues. Accordion with no visible borders. Questions in Space Grotesk, white. Open state: question turns orange, answer fades in below in Inter at 60% white opacity. Plus/minus icon rotates on open.

CTA: A single centered line of text in Anton, very large: "Ready to talk?" with an orange underline animation on hover. Below it, one button. No background color change — the dark continues. Minimal and confident.

Footer: Darkest section (#0A1628). Four columns in very muted text (white at 40% opacity). Links brighten to white on hover. The Carelabs logo sits alone at the top of the footer with generous space below it. Copyright bar at the very bottom in tiny text.

Overall: The entire page is 90% dark. Light only appears in the About section as a deliberate contrast moment. Typography does all the work — no images, no illustrations, no icons. Just type, space, and subtle motion. The page should feel like walking into a command center.

## Concept 2: "Split World"

Design a homepage built on a bold split-screen concept. Every section divides the viewport into two halves — one dark, one light — creating constant visual tension.

**Design direction:**

Hero: Screen split vertically. Left half: Dark Blue #094D76, right half: white. The headline spans both halves — starts in white text on the dark side, continues in dark text on the white side. The split line is not perfectly centered — it sits at about 45/55, creating asymmetry. The orange CTA button sits right on the split line, half on dark, half on light. Below the headline, small stats sit on the dark side, trust standards sit on the light side.

Services: Flip the split — now left is light, right is dark. Services are listed as a numbered vertical list on the dark right side. On the light left side, when you hover a service name, a large abstract geometric shape appears (a different shape per service — hexagon, circuit trace, wave form) drawn in thin blue lines. The shapes are decorative, not literal.

About section: Full-width white. Break from the split pattern — this section breathes. Centered text block with a large Anton headline and two paragraphs in Inter. Below: three value cards in a row, each with a diagonal blue-to-orange gradient on the left edge (not a straight border — an actual angled gradient strip at about 5 degrees).

Methodology: Return to split. Left dark, right light. The four steps stack vertically on the dark left side as large numbers (01-04) with titles. On the light right side, the description and detail for the currently active/visible step appears — this changes as the user scrolls (scroll-linked content swap using IntersectionObserver).

Blog: Full light section. Three article cards in a row with sharp design: each card has a solid colored top strip (alternating blue and orange), bold title, date, and a "Read" link. Cards have zero border radius — sharp corners everywhere. On hover: the top strip expands downward slightly (height transition) and the card lifts.

FAQ: Split again — but horizontal this time. Top half is dark with the section title in large white Anton. Bottom half is light with the accordion. The split line runs horizontally through the section, with the first question straddling the boundary.

CTA: Full dark blue (#094D76). Centered. Large headline. But the CTA button is a long horizontal bar (not a rounded button) — the full width of the content area, maybe 600px wide, with the text centered inside. Feels like pressing a physical control panel button.

Footer: Dark, four columns. The split concept appears one last time: a thin vertical orange line divides columns 2 and 3.

Overall: Asymmetry, tension, and contrast. Every section plays with dark vs light boundaries. The split creates visual energy without needing any images. Sharp corners throughout — zero border radius anywhere on the page.

## Concept 3: "The Grid"

Design a homepage inspired by engineering blueprints and technical drawings. A visible grid system becomes the design itself.

**Design direction:**

The entire page has a faint blueprint grid visible in the background — thin lines at about 3% opacity forming a precise grid (like graph paper). This grid is consistent across every section, giving the feeling that the entire page was designed on an engineering drawing board.

Hero: White background with the grid visible. The headline in massive Anton sits inside a "bounding box" — a thin blue dashed rectangle surrounds the text, like a selection box in CAD software. Corner marks (small L-shapes) at each corner of the bounding box. Below the headline, the description text with small red dimension lines pointing to it (like technical drawing annotations). The CTA buttons have sharp corners and thin borders. A small "SCALE 1:1" notation sits in the bottom-right corner of the hero in tiny gray text — an engineering drawing easter egg.

Trust bar: Standards displayed inside their own individual dashed bounding boxes, arranged in a precise row with exact spacing. Each box has small crosshair marks at center. Monospaced font for the standard names.

Services: A 3x2 grid where the grid lines are actually visible — blue lines forming the cell boundaries. Each cell contains: a technical drawing-style icon (thin line, geometric, blueprint aesthetic — NOT filled icons), the service title in Space Grotesk, and a one-line description. On hover: the cell's grid border turns orange and thickens slightly, and a small "DETAIL A" style callout appears in the corner.

About section: Text flows in two columns with a visible column gutter line. Pull quote on the left with quote marks styled as large thin blue angle brackets (« »). Stats at the bottom use a horizontal scale/ruler graphic — the numbers sit above tick marks on a measuring line, like a ruler or scale bar.

Methodology: Four steps connected by a flow line with right-angle turns (like a circuit diagram or piping diagram). Each step sits in a node box with rounded corners and a step number in a small circle at the top-left corner. Arrows indicate direction. The flow line is blue, the active step has an orange border.

Blog: Three article cards styled as "document sheets" — each card has a thin border, a title block in the top-left (small text: date, category), and the title in the body area. Small "REV. 01" text in the bottom corner. Like technical document cover sheets.

FAQ: Questions styled as "notes" on a drawing — each question has a triangle callout marker (like a technical drawing note bubble with a number) and the answer appears as the note content below.

CTA: Full-width section with a centered headline. The CTA button is styled as a technical drawing title block — rectangular, with the button text in the center cell and small decorative cells on either side containing tiny text like "REF: CTA-001" and today's date.

Footer: Grid continues. Four columns defined by visible grid lines. Information sits precisely within cells. The logo at the top has small dimension annotations around it showing the clearspace.

Overall: The engineering blueprint metaphor runs through everything but it's subtle and tasteful — never kitschy. The grid, dashed lines, dimension marks, and CAD-style annotations create a design that says "we are engineers" without using a single stock photo. Colors: mostly white with blue (#2575B6) grid lines and orange (#F15C30) for active states and accents only.

## Concept 4: "Kinetic Type"

Design a homepage where typography IS the design. No images, no illustrations, no icons. Pure typographic composition with motion.

**Design direction:**

Hero: White background. The headline is ENORMOUS — Anton at maybe 120px+ on desktop. It fills the full width of the screen and wraps to 2-3 lines. But each line is a different visual treatment: Line 1 in solid dark blue (#094D76). Line 2 in outlined/stroked text (transparent fill, blue stroke only). Line 3 in orange (#F15C30). Below the headline: the subtext is tiny in comparison — 14px Inter, sitting far below with lots of whitespace, creating extreme typographic contrast. One CTA button, small, orange, sitting quietly in the bottom-left of the hero — not centered.

Trust bar: The standard names scroll across the page as a slow horizontal marquee in massive light gray text (80px, 5% opacity). Over the top of this moving text, the section label "Trusted Standards" sits still in small orange caps. The contrast between the giant moving background text and the small static label creates visual interest.

Services: Each service gets a single line. The service name in Space Grotesk at about 48px fills the full width. Below each name is a 1px line. On hover: the text shifts from black to blue, a brief description slides in from the right (revealed via clip-path), and the bottom line turns orange. Just typography and lines — nothing else.

About section: A large text block set in Inter at 24px with generous line height (1.8). Within the paragraph, key phrases are highlighted — set in Space Grotesk bold and colored blue. The effect is like reading a highlighted document. Below: stats as inline text, not separate blocks. Like: "Across 50+ countries and 1000+ projects, we've spent 15+ years engineering certainty." The numbers are in Anton, orange, inline with the Inter body text.

Methodology: The four steps are set as a single continuous paragraph of text with large step numbers (Anton, orange, 60px) interrupting the flow. "01 We assess your facility's electrical infrastructure. 02 We model fault scenarios using IEEE standards. 03 We deliver detailed compliance reports. 04 We provide ongoing support." The numbers break the visual rhythm and create a reading experience rather than a scannable grid.

Blog: Three articles as simple text stacks. Title in Space Grotesk at 28px bold. Date below in tiny gray Inter. A 1px orange line below each article separates them. No cards, no borders, no backgrounds. Just text in space.

FAQ: Questions in large Space Grotesk (24px). Answers in smaller Inter (16px) at 50% opacity, indented. No accordion — all questions and answers visible, creating a long-form readable Q&A section. Questions are in dark blue, answers in gray.

CTA: A single line of text in Anton at 80px+: "Let's talk." Left-aligned, not centered. Orange. Below it: "info@carelabz.com" in small Inter. Below that: the phone number. Below that: "Dubai, UAE". Just text. Nothing else.

Footer: Dense typographic footer. All four columns' content runs together in a continuous block of small text separated by " / " dividers and line breaks. Almost like code or a terminal readout. Very compact, very intentional.

Overall: This is design by typography alone. The hierarchy, the contrast between huge Anton headlines and small Inter body text, the interplay between static and moving text, the use of color within text (not on backgrounds) — all of it creates visual richness without a single non-text element. This concept says "we don't need decoration to be impressive."

## Concept 5: "Scroll Theater"

Design a homepage that transforms as you scroll — sections morph, overlap, and transition into each other rather than stacking as separate blocks.

**Design direction:**

Hero: Full viewport, Dark Blue #094D76. The headline and CTAs are centered. As the user scrolls down, the hero doesn't just scroll away — it shrinks and moves upward, becoming the sticky navbar. The dark background compresses into the nav bar while the content area reveals a white page beneath. The transition is smooth (CSS position: sticky + scale transform). The hero headline shrinks into the navbar as a subtle tagline.

Trust bar: As the hero compresses away, the trust bar fades in from below — the standards appear one by one, left to right, with a staggered delay (each standard animates in 100ms after the previous). They sit on a white background with subtle blue underlines.

Services: The service section starts with a large centered title. As you scroll INTO this section, the title stays pinned at the top (sticky) while the service rows scroll up beneath it. Each service row slides in from the right side of the screen (translateX animation triggered by IntersectionObserver). The services stack vertically with generous spacing. Each service has: orange number, Space Grotesk title, Inter description. When a service row reaches the center of the viewport, it briefly enlarges (scale 1.02) and its number turns from orange to blue — then it continues scrolling up. The pinned section title at the top shows "Services" with a dynamic counter that updates: "Services (3/6)" as you scroll through them.

About section: This section uses parallax — the background image (a subtle abstract gradient mesh in blues and oranges, very blurred) scrolls at 50% speed while the text scrolls normally on top. Two text columns with a large centered stat that counts up when in view. The stat number starts at 0 and counts to the target number over 1.5 seconds.

Methodology: The four steps are laid out as a horizontal strip that scrolls HORIZONTALLY while the user scrolls vertically. The section takes up about 3 viewport heights worth of vertical scroll, but the visual movement is horizontal — the steps slide from right to left across the screen. Each step is a tall card (full viewport height within the section) with a large step number, title, and description. A progress bar at the bottom of the section fills from left to right as you scroll through the four steps. This is the scroll-linked horizontal scroll pattern.

Blog: Three article cards overlap each other slightly — like fanned-out cards in a hand. As you scroll, they un-fan and spread out into a proper row. The animation is: starts stacked with 30% overlap and slight rotation, transitions to flat grid with zero overlap. Triggered by scroll position.

FAQ: The accordion questions cascade in — each one drops in from above with a slight bounce (CSS spring animation). They don't all appear at once; they sequence in as you scroll, creating a "building the page as you read it" feeling.

CTA: Simple, but with a twist — the entire section background color transitions from white to Dark Blue #094D76 as you scroll into it. Not a sharp color boundary — an actual smooth gradient transition (CSS background-position shift). Text starts dark and transitions to white as the background darkens.

Footer: Static and clean — after all the motion above, the footer is deliberately calm. Four columns, dark background, no animation. It's the quiet ending after the show.

Overall: Every section transitions into the next — nothing just "starts" or "stops." The page is one continuous experience. Scroll position drives everything: hero compression, horizontal movement, parallax, card fanning, background color transitions, counter animations. The page rewards scrolling — there's always something happening. But it never feels chaotic because each animation is smooth, purposeful, and uses the same easing curve (cubic-bezier(0, 0.55, 0.45, 1)).
